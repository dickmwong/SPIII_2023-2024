Q1. Define \(\widehat{m}(u) := \mathbb{E}\left[ \sum_{n \ge 0} e^{-u S_n}\right] = \sum_{n \ge 0} \mathbb{E}\left[ e^{-u S_n}\right]\). Assuming that \(\mathbb{P}(X_k = 0) < 1\), explain why \(\widehat{m}(u) < \infty\) for every \(u > 0\), and show that $$\lim_{u\to 0^+} u \widehat{m}(u) = \frac{1}{\mu}.$$ (Hint: express \(\widehat{m}(u)\) in terms of \(\mathbb{E}[e^{-u S_0}]\) and \(\mathbb{E}[e^{-u X}]\) where \(X\) has the same distribution as the i.i.d. increments \((X_n)_{n \ge 0}\).)

Q2. Suppose \((M(t))_{t \ge 0}\) is a zero-delayed renewal process with a continuous distribution \(F_X\) for interarrival times (i.e. \(F_X(x) = \int_0^x f_X(s) ds\) for some probability density function \(f_X(\cdot)\)).

  1. Show that for any \(t \ge 0\), the function \(H(t) = m(t) - \frac{t}{\mu}\) satisfies $$H(t) = h(t) + \int_0^t H(t-s) f_X(s)ds \qquad \left(\text{or equivalently} \quad H(t) = h(t) + \mathbb{E}[H(t-X)]\right)$$ for any \(t \ge 0\), where \(h(t) =\frac{1}{\mu}\int_t^\infty [1-F_X(s)]ds\).
  2. Find \(H(t)\) when the interarrival times follow \(\mathrm{Exp}(1/\mu)\) distribution.
