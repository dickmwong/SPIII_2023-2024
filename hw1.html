Q2. Establish the conditional monotone convergence theorem using the definition of abstract conditional expectation: let \(X_n\) be a sequence of non-negative random variables such that \(X_n \le X_{n+1}\) almost surely for all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), and \(X = \lim_{n \to \infty} X_n\) be integrable, then for any sub-\(\sigma\)-algebra \(\mathcal{G}\) we have \[\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{G}] = \mathbb{E}[X|\mathcal{G}]\qquad a.s. \end{equation*}\] Hint:

  • First explain why \(0 \le \mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{G}] \le \mathbb{E}[X_{n+1} | \mathcal{G}]\) for every \(n\). (This is important because it shows, in particular, that \(\lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{G}]\) exists almost surely as a monotone limit.)
  • Conclude the proof by showing that \(\lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{G}]\) is equal to \(\mathbb{E}[X|\mathcal{G}]\) using the definition of abstract conditional expectation, i.e. \[\begin{equation*} \mathbb{E}\left[ \left(\lim_{n \to \infty} \mathbb{E}[X_n | \mathcal{G}]\right) 1_A\right] = \mathbb{E}\left[ \left( \mathbb{E}[X|\mathcal{G}]\right) 1_A\right] \qquad \forall A \in \mathcal{G}. \end{equation*}\]

  • Recall the moment generating function of \((X_1, X_2)\), and show that \(\widetilde{X}_1\) is independent of \(X_2\).
  • Using basic properties of (abstract) conditional expectation and evaluate the desired quantity.
