Q1. Let \((X_n)_{n\ge 1}\) and \((Y_n)_{n\ge 1}\) be two (homogeneous) branching processes starting from \(X_0=1, Y_0=1\), and such that \(X_1 \le_{\mathrm{st}} Y_1\). Show that \(X_n \le_{\mathrm{st}} Y_n\) for all \(n\ge 0\).

Q2. Recall that the total variation distance between two random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) is defined as $$ d_{\mathrm{TV}}(X, Y) := d_{\mathrm{TV}}(\mu_X, \mu_Y) := \sup_{A} |\mu_X(A) - \mu_X(Y)| = \sup_A |\mathbb{P}(X \in A) - \mathbb{P}(Y \in A)|. $$

  1. Check that \(d_{\mathrm{TV}}\) is non-negative, symmetric (i.e. \(d_{\mathrm{TV}}(X,Y)=d_{\mathrm{TV}}(Y,X)\)) and satisfies the triangle inequality (i.e. \(d_{\mathrm{TV}}(X,Z)\le d_{\mathrm{TV}}(X,Y)+d_{\mathrm{TV}}(Y,Z)\) for any random variables \(X, Y, Z\)).
  2. Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be integer-valued random variables with probability mass functions \(\{p_k\}_{k \in \mathbb{Z}}\) and \(\{q_k\}_{k \in \mathbb{Z}}\) respectively. Show that the equalities $$ d_{\mathrm{TV}}(X,Y) =\frac{1}{2}\sum_{k\in \mathbb{Z}}|p_k-q_k| =\sum_{k\in \mathbb{Z}} (p_k-\min(p_k,q_k)) =\sum_{k\in \mathbb{Z}} (q_k-\min(p_k,q_k)) $$ hold.
